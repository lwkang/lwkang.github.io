About me

My name is Liuwang Kang and I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Virginia, working with Prof. Haiying (Helen) Shen at the University of Virginia and Prof. Rafael Diaz at Virginia Modeling, Analysis & Simulation Center (VMASC). I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of Virginia in August 2021, under the supervision of Prof. Haiying (Helen) Shen. Before that, I received the B.E. degree from Chang’an University, and the M.S. degree from Clemson University. My research interests are in the Internet-of-Things and autonomous driving with a focus on driving safety, security and privacy analysis.

Cover driving security, privacy and safety topics in intelligent transportation systems

Attack Detection and Mitigation for Vehicle Security

Modern vehicles communicate with outside-vehicle environments through Bluetooth or WiFi and become vulnerable to cyber attacks and sensor attacks. How to detect these cyber and sensor attacks for ensuring vehicle security becomes necessary.
ICDCS’19 ICCCN’20 MASS’21

Smartphone-based Battery Attack and Driving Path Inference for Electric Vehicles

Electric vehicles become more popular in recent years and many services (e.g., smartphone apps and battery charging stations are allowed to control and monitor batteries in electric vehicles) are developed to make vehicle usage more convenient. These services make battery relevant information vulnerable to security threats causing driving pravicy issues.
ICCPS’19 IPSN’20

Control Policy Extraction and Control Decision-Making for Autonomous Vehicles

Autonomous vehicle technologies have been adopted by many autonomous companies to reduce traffic congestion and improve driving safety. An autonomous vehicle follows its control policies and makes real-time decisions based on measured signals from sensors during a driving process. How to extact control policies and determing optimal control decisions affects autonomous driving performance greatly.
MASS’21 SECON’21 SEC’21
